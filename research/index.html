Research

An energy function defines a physical system over a state space that determines the distribution of states. In my work, the states are images and the energy function is a ConvNet. My current research has two major branches:

Learning Energy Functions of High-Dimensional Data

Energy-based learning with ConvNet functions exhibits surprising behaviors not encountered for earlier energy functions. My research identifies two major families of outcomes: convergent learning and non-convergent learning.

Mapping Mascropic Structures of High-Dimensional Energy Functions

The local modes of an energy function are stable states that appear with high probability. Related groups of local modes form macroscopic non-convex structures that are analagous to folding funnels of protein potentials. My work uses a novel MCMC algorithm to detect metastable structures of learned energy functions that correspond to intuitive image concepts.
